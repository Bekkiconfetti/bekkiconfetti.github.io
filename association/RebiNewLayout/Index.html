A Dado as your agent

Imagine you're a pop star and you don't want to discuss ticket prices for the next concert because you are busy writing that life-changing ballade, that will be the anthem of a generation.
What do you do? You hire an agent to handle things for you!

Your dado is like your automatic agent, who represents you in the digital realm - only in most cases, you haven't really discussed your interests or given them any guidelines on how you would like them to do this. That's what this agency agreement is for. So next time when your dado is interacting with your bank, your health insurance, tinder date, or dry cleaner on your behalf, they'll know what details they have permission to share and what you would like to keep private.

What third party interests would you like to support?

There are two things, that make interacting with your Dado a tricky business: their fragmented nature and the fact that it's very hard to meet them on your own terms.

Both in the process of data collection and in its representation back to you there are usually other players involved. Currently, these third parties are in a very powerful position, because they select what information about you is important to record and how this information is presented back to you. Additionally, they also decide what doesn't get relayed back to you and what other interests your data is used for without your direct involvement.

What data are we talking about?

Dados are not conventional species with a central nervous system but are usually scattered around many different servers and databases. Often the only link between these fragments is their reference to you. This agreement automatically extends to every fraction of their existence, meaning all digital information that exists about you, generated with and without your knowledge.

This includes data collected by online services, social media platforms, smart wearables, smart home devices but also public CCTV cameras, border control officers, etc.

Metadata and predictions that can be derived from online and offline activities are also within the scope of the agreement.

Who are the third parties?

The set of third parties your dado interacts are quite diverse to say the least, ranging from governing bodies to private companies in all different types of fields.

The Association of Human & Dado Friendship publishes a yearly ranking of these third parties, where they are evaluated by a panel of experts according to criteria such as transparency over data use, transparency over storage of data, data greediness.

Taking in account this ranking, your answers about the interests you would be willing to support and your classification of the different data types, we have pre-assigned third parties to the different ranks of sharing.
